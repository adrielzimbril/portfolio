### The Context

In Ivory Coast, entrepreneurship is bustling, but moving to e-commerce remains an uphill battle.

International solutions are often:
- **Costly** and ill-suited to local currencies.
- **Disconnected** from payment realities (Mobile Money).
- **Complex** for a non-technical user.

Deewid was born to break these barriers and offer a sovereign and intuitive alternative.

### The Challenge

How to hide technical complexity (databases, servers, APIs) behind an interface that speaks the entrepreneur's language?

The challenge was to transform "management software" into a **growth tool**. We had to reduce the mental friction of the "setup" so that the user could focus solely on their products.

### Our Solution: The Magic of Immediacy

We reversed the classic paradigm. Instead of configuring to see, we make them **see to configure**.

From registration, the shop exists. The user sees their name on a professional website and just has to "fill in the boxes".
- **Minimalist forms**: Anticipating needs to reduce data entry.
- **2-click payments**: Native integration of local solutions (Orange, MTN, Wave).
- **Visual feedback**: Every change is visible in real-time.

### Impact & Perspectives

In a few months, Deewid has allowed artisans and creators to take the digital leap.

The transition from a 45-minute setup to only **12 minutes** is not just a UX performance statistic. It is proof that design can truly **unlock economic opportunities** for those who need them most.
