### The Context

The Weseemba project was born from a simple yet alarming observation: while African creativity shines globally in music and fashion, digital tools for creators remained desperately anchored in Western standards. I joined the adventure at its inception in March 2025, first as a freelancer and then as a full-time Product Designer to structure this ambitious vision from Abidjan.

### The Challenge

The challenge was twofold. Technically, we had to integrate AI services (voice and image generation) seamlessly into a complex SaaS environment. Creatively, we had to avoid the trap of the "classic stock library" — often cold and utilitarian — to offer an experience more akin to visiting a contemporary art gallery. How to make a voice-over search as exciting as an exhibition?

### Our Approach

As Product Designer, my major decision was to adopt a radical artistic direction: the **Dark Art Gallery**. By using deep backgrounds and minimalist yet characterful typography, we created a showcase that gives full space to image and sound.

We also rethought AI integration. Instead of having separate AI widgets, we infused them into the natural workflow. For example, when searching for talent, the AI suggests complementary musical or visual moods based on the project's cultural context, rather than just tags. This is what we call **AI Cultural Awareness**.

### Impact & Results

Today, Weseemba is more than just a platform: it's a catalyst. My 13-month contribution helped take it from a raw concept to a finished, stable product recognized for its elegance. Success is measured not just in user numbers, but in the pride of African creators who finally have a tool that looks like them and amplifies their voice without distorting it.
